Mission History

A chronological look at key moments in NASA's rover program, from the first successful landing at Ares Vallis in 1997 to the ongoing science campaigns of Curiosity and Perseverance. Dates below use Earth calendar years; Martian timing is usually reported in "sols," or Martian days.

Launch & Landing Timeline

  1. December 4, 1996

    Mars Pathfinder Launches

    The Mars Pathfinder spacecraft, carrying the Sojourner rover, lifts off from Cape Canaveral aboard a Delta II rocket, beginning a seven-month cruise to Mars.

  2. July 4, 1997

    Sojourner Lands at Ares Vallis

    Pathfinder touches down using an airbag-cushioned bouncing landing. Sojourner rolls off onto the surface days later, becoming the first wheeled vehicle to operate on another planet.

  3. September 27, 1997

    Sojourner Mission Ends

    After operating for 83 sols (roughly twelve times its planned mission length) the Pathfinder lander loses contact. Sojourner returned over 550 images and analyzed more than fifteen rocks.

  4. November 26, 2011

    Curiosity Launches

    The Mars Science Laboratory spacecraft, carrying the car-sized Curiosity rover, launches aboard an Atlas V rocket, beginning an eight-and-a-half-month journey to Mars.

  5. August 6, 2012

    Curiosity Lands in Gale Crater

    Curiosity becomes the first rover to use the "sky crane" descent system, lowered to the surface on cables by a rocket-powered descent stage. The landing is nicknamed the "Seven Minutes of Terror."

  6. July 30, 2020

    Perseverance Launches

    Perseverance, along with the Ingenuity helicopter, launches aboard an Atlas V rocket. The mission is the first to carry equipment designed to collect and cache samples for eventual return to Earth.

  7. February 18, 2021

    Perseverance Lands in Jezero Crater

    The rover touches down in an ancient river delta, again using the sky crane system. Jezero Crater was selected for its rich potential to preserve biosignatures from Mars's warmer, wetter past.

  8. April 19, 2021

    Ingenuity's First Flight

    The Ingenuity helicopter completes the first powered, controlled flight on another planet, hovering above the Martian surface for about 40 seconds.

  9. Present Day

    Ongoing Exploration

    Curiosity continues its climb up Mount Sharp inside Gale Crater, while Perseverance continues to cache rock samples in Jezero Crater for a future Mars Sample Return mission.
